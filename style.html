What I do

Focus areas

Three pillars that describe most of my work - what I design, how I model it, and where I want to apply it.

Mechanical and phononic metamaterials

Architected mechanical lattices that shape vibration and sound across a wide frequency range.

  • Su-Schrieffer-Heeger type chains and ladders with engineered band gaps.
  • Localized edge and domain wall states, including strongly perturbed regimes.
  • Long range and beyond nearest neighbor interactions in realistic structures.

Devices, packaging and heat

Applying metamaterial ideas to devices and package like stacks, with thermal and mechanical constraints.

  • Phononic concepts for chip level heat spreading and reliability.
  • Thermo mechanical analysis of stacked device and package architectures.
  • Experience with semiconductor devices and packaging courses and training.

Complex systems and data

Using data and statistical methods when the physics and the system scale demand it.

  • Machine learning for soil properties and accident severity prediction.
  • Spatial and spatio temporal models for environmental and biomedical data.
  • Text and social data analysis, including hate speech detection pipelines.
