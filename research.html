Research overview

Mechanical and phononic metamaterials

My research focuses on architected mechanical structures that control vibration and acoustic waves. I work across theory, simulation, and experiment, with an emphasis on topological phenomena and realistic device and package constraints.

Topological phononic lattices

One dimensional and quasi one dimensional lattices that support robust localized modes.

  • Su Schrieffer Heeger chains and ladders with tunable unit cells.
  • In gap edge and domain wall states under strong perturbations.
  • Effect of long range coupling on band topology and winding numbers.

Bulk mode.

Interface mode.

Waveguides and energy routing

Using band gaps and topological states to control where waves travel in a structure.

  • Guided modes along interfaces and defects in phononic crystals.
  • Transition from extended to localized modes under geometry or material changes.
  • Analysis of robustness to disorder and fabrication variation.

Nonlinear and amplitude dependent effects

How nonlinearity modifies dispersion, localization, and energy transfer.

  • Mass in mass chains with nonlinear springs and internal resonators.
  • Multiple scale style analysis and numerical time integration.
  • Frequency shifts and new branches that appear with amplitude.
Methods

How I work

I combine analytical derivations, numerical solvers, and experiments, and I try to keep the same problem flowing through all three levels.

Analytical models

I start from minimal models that capture the key physics and give intuition.

  • Dispersion relations for periodic chains and beams.
  • Eigenvalue problems for finite lattices and edge states.
  • Asymptotic and perturbation methods when nonlinearity is present.

Finite elements and numerics

I use numerical solvers when geometry, materials, or boundary conditions become complex.

  • COMSOL and other FEM tools for band structures and mode shapes.
  • Custom MATLAB and Python codes for large chains and parametric sweeps.
  • Time domain simulations and FFT based spectro spatial analysis.

Experiments

I aim to close the loop with measurements whenever possible.

  • 3D printing of mechanical and phononic samples.
  • Laser vibrometry for full field vibration measurements.
  • Comparison of measured and predicted dispersion and mode localization.
Current topics

Active research directions

Examples of current and recent questions

  • How far can we perturb an SSH style lattice and still retain useful interface modes.
  • What happens to the usual winding number picture when we include long range interactions.
  • How temperature, geometry, and small scale effects shift phonon bands in graphene like systems.
  • How phononic or architected layers can help manage heat and stress in device and package stacks.
